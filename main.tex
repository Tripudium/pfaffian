\begin{abstract}
We prove bounds for the volume of tubular neighbourhoods of sets defined by Pfaffian functions, generalising known results for algebraic varieties. The bounds are given in terms of the Pfaffian format of the defining functions and are derived by combining Weyl's tube formula with degree bounds on the generalised Gauss map obtained via Khovanskii's theorem. As an application, we obtain tail bounds on the probability distribution of a condition number measuring the robustness of neural network classifiers with Pfaffian activation functions, in both the uniform and Gaussian settings. The resulting bounds decay as $O(1/t)$ and depend explicitly on the network depth, width, and activation format.
\end{abstract}
